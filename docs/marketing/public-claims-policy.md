# Public Claims Policy

Public copy must describe CCAI outputs as screening signals, evidence records, or review support artifacts. It must not describe CCAI output as final legal advice, a regulatory determination, or a replacement for the customer's compliance judgment.

## Required framing

- CCAI is screening infrastructure that supports regulated teams.
- CCAI produces evidence-backed screening results and review cases.
- Customers remain responsible for final compliance decisions, regulator responses, and legal interpretation.
- Determinism claims must be scoped to the same canonical input, tenant configuration, rules, and data snapshot.
- Isolation claims must describe enforced tenant boundaries and tests, not impossibility.

## Disallowed framing

- Absolute accuracy, compliance, security, or outcome guarantees.
- Claims that cross-tenant access is impossible.
- Claims that CCAI provides legal, regulatory, or compliance advice.
- Claims that AI-generated explanations or adverse media summaries can be relied on without human review.

## Why this exists

The marketing site is a public representation of the product. Claim safety protects customers, preserves the system-of-record boundary, and keeps public copy aligned with the engine's modular architecture.
